Esperanza Collado is a Spanish artist-researcher. Her 16mm film projection-based performance-environments and critical writings investigate phenomenological and situational aspects of cinema in its spacial, sculptural, social and choreographic dimensions. Her book Paracinema: la desmaterialización del cine en las prácticas artísticas ("Paracinema: the dematerialisation of film in artistic practices") received the Spanish Writings on Art Prize from Fundación Arte y Derecho in 2011. She co-founded Experimental Film Club in Dublin (Ireland) with Aoife Desmond, Donal Foreman and Alan Lampert. Together with sound artist Rafael Martínez del Pozo she founded the record label LEVE, which released 8 site-specific field recordings editions on 45rpm vinyl made by invited artists. She lectures in the Fine Arts College in Cuenca and in Elías Querejeta Zine Eskola (San Sebastian), in Spain. She is currently on a Fellowship in Taiwan researching expanded cinema in East Asia.
