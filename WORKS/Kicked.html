→ Digital documentation 		Kicked with the Front Foot on the Dark Side of the Deck is an expanded cinema performance that treats the cinematic event as a manifestation of energy, movement, bodily tensions and what I call "mutant installations". Taking the subculture and physical vocabulary of skateboarding as a conceptual starting point, the work explores the parallels between the “grind” of the board and the mechanical friction of the film strip passing through the projector.

This project originated within the pedagogical context of my experimental film classes, emerging from a shared investigation into the mechanics of skateboarding and the spatial potentialities of the film apparatus.

As explored in my theoretical research, Kicked operates through what I define as “gestures-montage” (gestos-montaje). Here, the editing does not occur on a timeline, but in the physical space through the artist’s actions. These gestures create “mutant installations” made of objects and light matter, where the cinematic emerges as a live event rather than being contained within a static display.
